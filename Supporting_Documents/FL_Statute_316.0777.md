# Florida Statute 316.0777
## Automated License Plate Recognition Systems; Installation Within Rights-of-Way of State Highway System; Public Records Exemption

**Source:** [Florida Statutes - Section 316.0777](https://www.leg.state.fl.us/Statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0300-0399/0316/Sections/0316.0777.html)

---

## (1) Definitions

As used in this section, the term:

### (a) Active, Criminal Intelligence Information, and Criminal Investigative Information

"Active," "criminal intelligence information," and "criminal investigative information" have the same meanings as provided in s. 119.011(3).

### (b) Agency

"Agency" has the same meaning as provided in s. 119.011.

### (c) Automated License Plate Recognition System

"Automated license plate recognition system" means a system of one or more mobile or fixed high-speed cameras combined with computer algorithms to convert images of license plates into computer-readable data.

### (d) Criminal Justice Agency

"Criminal justice agency" has the same meaning as provided in s. 119.011.

---

## (2) Installation Within State Highway System Rights-of-Way

### (a) Definition of Law Enforcement Agency

As used in this subsection, the term "law enforcement agency" means an agency that has a primary mission of preventing and detecting crime and enforcing state penal, criminal, traffic, and motor vehicle laws and, in furtherance of that mission, employs law enforcement officers as defined in s. 943.10(1).

### (b) Installation Requirements and Restrictions

At the discretion of the Department of Transportation, an automated license plate recognition system may be installed within the right-of-way, as defined in s. 334.03(21), of a road on the State Highway System when installed at the request of a law enforcement agency for the purpose of collecting active criminal intelligence information or active criminal investigative information as defined in s. 119.011(3). 

An automated license plate recognition system may not be used to issue a notice of violation for a traffic infraction or a uniform traffic citation. 

Such installation must be in accordance with placement and installation guidelines developed by the Department of Transportation. An automated license plate recognition system must be removed within 30 days after the Department of Transportation notifies the requesting law enforcement agency that such removal must occur.

### (c) Financial Responsibility and Liability

Installation and removal of an automated license plate recognition system are at the sole expense of the requesting law enforcement agency. The Department of Transportation is not liable for any damages caused to any person by the requesting law enforcement agency's operation of such system.

### (d) Data Retention Limits

Records containing images and data generated through the use of an automated license plate recognition system may not be retained longer than the maximum period provided in the retention schedule established pursuant to s. 316.0778.

---

## (3) Confidential and Exempt Information

The following information held by an agency is confidential and exempt from s. 119.07(1) and s. 24(a), Art. I of the State Constitution:

### (a) Images and Data with Personal Identifying Information

Images and data containing or providing personal identifying information obtained through the use of an automated license plate recognition system.

### (b) Personal Identifying Information in Generated Data

Personal identifying information of an individual in data generated or resulting from images obtained through the use of an automated license plate recognition system.

---

## (4) Permitted Disclosures

Such information may be disclosed as follows:

### (a) Disclosure to Criminal Justice Agencies

Any such information may be disclosed by or to a criminal justice agency in the performance of the criminal justice agency's official duties.

### (b) Disclosure to Individual License Plate Registrant

Any such information relating to a license plate registered to an individual may be disclosed to the individual, unless such information constitutes active criminal intelligence information or active criminal investigative information.

---

## (5) Retroactive Applicability

This exemption applies to such information held by an agency before, on, or after the effective date of this exemption.

## Key Points Summary

### For Law Enforcement Agencies:

**Installation Authority:**
- May request ALPR installation in State Highway System rights-of-way at DOT's discretion
- Must be for collecting active criminal intelligence or investigative information
- Cannot be used to issue traffic citations or notices of violation
- Must comply with DOT placement and installation guidelines

**Financial Responsibility:**
- Agency bears all costs of installation and removal
- DOT has no liability for damages from agency's system operation

**Data Retention:**
- Must comply with retention schedule in § 316.0778
- Cannot retain data longer than maximum period allowed

### For the Public:

**Public Records Exemption:**
- ALPR images and data with personal identifying information are confidential and exempt
- Personal identifying information in ALPR-generated data is protected
- Individuals may request their own license plate information unless it's part of active criminal intelligence or investigation

**Prohibited Uses:**
- ALPR systems cannot be used for automated traffic enforcement
- Cannot be used to issue traffic infractions or citations

### For Department of Transportation:

**Discretionary Authority:**
- Has discretion to approve or deny installation requests
- Develops placement and installation guidelines
- May require removal with 30-day notice
- Not liable for damages from law enforcement operation

## Comparison to Original Legislation

This statute was created by **Chapter 2014-170** (CS/CS/HB 599), which originally established the public records exemption for ALPR data. Key amendments include:

- **2019-16:** Added provisions for installation in DOT rights-of-way and restrictions on use
- **2023-70:** Updated data retention cross-reference to § 316.0778
